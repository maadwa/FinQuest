Understanding the Stock Market

The stock market is a complex ecosystem where investors buy and sell ownership shares in publicly traded companies. Understanding how it works is essential for making informed investment decisions.

Stock markets function as both primary markets (where new securities are issued) and secondary markets (where existing securities are traded). They provide companies with access to capital and offer investors opportunities to grow their wealth over time.

Key stock market concepts you'll learn in this module include:

  • How stocks are bought and sold through exchanges
  • What market indices like the S&P 500 and Dow Jones represent
  • The roles of brokers, market makers, and other market participants
  • How market sentiment and economic factors influence stock prices

Types of Stock Analysis

When evaluating stocks, investors typically use two primary approaches: fundamental analysis and technical analysis. Each offers a different perspective on a stock's potential value and future performance.

Fundamental Analysis examines a company's financial health, competitive position, industry trends, and growth prospects to determine its intrinsic value. This approach looks at factors such as:

  • Financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow)
  • Revenue growth and profit margins
  • Price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio and other valuation metrics
  • Competitive advantages and market position

Technical Analysis focuses on statistical trends from trading activity, such as price movements and volume. This approach uses:

  • Chart patterns and trend lines
  • Moving averages and momentum indicators
  • Support and resistance levels
  • Volume analysis to confirm price movements
